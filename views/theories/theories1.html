Theories as of chapter 6

Apr 25th 2022 3 Min read

I am six chapters in, about 130 pages through this 1000+ page book. I think it is fitting for me to start formulating some theories. But before I go on, I have to confess I am not going through this story completely blind. I have seen movies 2 and 3 of the Lord of the Rings trilogy (The Two Towers and Return of the King) about 2 decades ago. I have also read The Hobbit about 1 decade ago.

My memory is pretty hazy on the finer details but I do know Frodo succeeds in destroying the ring by throwing it into Mount Doom. I know that Gollum jumps after it. At some point Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli shows up. Gandalf does cool wizard shit. Also epic battles between orcs and the our side. That's about it. That's all I remember. Wait no. Also legolas riding stairs with a shield like a surfboard while shooting arrows. I imagine that was not in the books, but that was pretty epic to third grade me. With that in mind, I know the good guys win, but I do not know the finer details.

1) Where is Gandalf? He promised to meet Frodo before the hobbits left on their journey. Gandalf seems to be a man of his wonder so I believe something has befallen him. I hypothesize that he is entangled with the Enemy. Sauron's forces may have captured or weakened him so he cannot accompany Frodo on this treacherous journey. My other theory, his eyebrows have grow so thick and fuzzy that they have completely covered his eyes. He cannot not see back to the Shire and Middle-earth has not yet adopted the practice of plucking eyebrows.

2) Is Bilbo dead? After Bilbo relinquished the ring to Frodo he left on a journey outside the Shire. The ring gave Bilbo basically eternal life at the cost of his 'soul' or 'essence'. Now that Biblo no longer possesses the ring on him, does the ring's magic linger or is he completely mortal again? I believe that Bilbo is dead. After a few days journeying, his overripe body succumbs to the harsh elements. No longer feeling the spry psuedo-youth powers the ring gave him, Bilbo finds himself a final resting place, content with passing away in a land beyond the Shire like he once adventured back so many years ago.

3) Who is Tom Bombadil? Who is this mysterious not-human, not-dwarf, not-hobbit, not-elf person? He claims to be beyond ancient, before other living things existed on Middle-earth, before even the rain drops or trees were there. His claims are ridculous, but if they were true, it be similar to tales of Norse mythology - Did Tom Bombadil create Middle-earth himself?
I have been informed that Tom is not in the movies. Yet, someone so powerful as to not be affected by the ring and yet see someone hidden by the ring must have powers that could rival Sauron himself if much of Sauron's power and magic is linked to the ring. So someone this powerful does not play a large role in destroying the ring that he can be written out of the movies plot? What a curious narrative.
But I believe we have not seen the last of him in the book. At the end of Chapter 6, Tom makes a point to teach the hobbits about a song that will summon him when they are in need. Another Chekov's gun has been shown, let us see when it will be fired.

Allan Gao
