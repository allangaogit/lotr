Notes from L

Not to give anything away, but to expand on some of your confused points. Sauron is a Maiar and essentially a lesser god. His physical entity can be destroyed, but his essence (soul/divinity/whatever you want to call it) can not be. Even Morgoth/Melkor is still in existence even though he is in the Void. Not to give anything too drastic away, but the Ring essentially holds a lot of his being in it, so if it were to be destroyed he would have to retreat back to Valinor and more or less become whole again before he could ever return to Middle-earth

Celebrimbor was an elf that Sauron came to and manipulated/deceived into creating the rings of power. Once all the other rings were made, Sauron more or less watched and learned from Celebrimbors technique and created his own.

The White Council going to Mirkwood is depicted in the Hobbit movies. You may remember the area being called Dol Guldur. Sauron basically takes over a land and uses it to hide himself and his schemes. He was not strong enough to move to Mordor at this point. (but at the time they call him the Necromancer cause they weren't exactly sure)

Oh and Gandalf does not want to touch the ring because he is also a Maiar and he can basically feel the malice in the Ring

The other rings do have special things they offer the wearer, but it mostly just enhances your self. i dont remember exactly the details on the abilities, but the Ring doesn't really make you invisible technically speaking
it just basically shows/puts you in like the "shadow" world of the world
thats why when you wear it Sauron and the Ring Wraiths know exactly where you are. because they are also The Shadow
