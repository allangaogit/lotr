Three is Company

Apr 18th 2022 pages 65-86

Gonna list names of people and places that I think are going to be important.

Rivendell - elvish community - home to Elrond Halfelven (If I recall correctly, the agent smith looking elf)

Gandalf senses something is afoot. He leaves Frodo to investigate and promises to come back before Frodo's 50th birthday. The day that Frodo plans to leave the shire to embark on his quest. Gandalf does not return. This is highly unusual

Frodo's friends: Peregrin (Pippin) Took, Meriadoc (Merry) Brandybuck

I don't think Samwise (Sam) Gamgee counts as Frodo's good friend yet. He works as a landscaper for Bag's End

The chapter title references Frodo, Pippin, and Sam leaving Bag's End to help Frodo move houses after he sold Bag's End

Even after starting the journey and the trio of hobbits are still in the Shire, they realize they are being tracked or possibly hunted by a mysterious hooded black rider

Frodo subconciously feels like the rider is specifically searching for him and he is compelled to wear the ring

After a second close encounter with a black rider the trio meet a group a wandering high elves, they say they are exiles and plan to return over the great sea (leaving Middle Earth?)

The leader of the high elves introduces himself, Gildor Inglorian of house Finrod. He is impressed by Frodo's limited knowledge of elvish tongue and is aware that the 'Enemy' is after Frodo for one reason or another. Which is highly unsual for hobbits to be invovled in the greater affairs of the world

The book implies the Enemy is Sauron. Is Sauron the most powerful evil this world has ever seen? Or is he just the biggest threat to Middle Earth that exists at the this time and there has been greater threats in the past that have been either vanquished and forgotten?

I digress. The high elves accompany the hobbits to Woodhall, a eastern village of the Shire. There they exchange pleasantries, drinks, and information concerning the journey

It is a very perilous journey and dark forces have been moving in the shadows. A lot of responsibility falls on a person so small
