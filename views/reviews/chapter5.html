A Conspiracy Unmasked

Apr 20th 2022 pages 98-108

Hobbits arrive at Bucklebury of Brandy Hall.

Is Pippin the bard of the group? He's always singing a song: about traveling, going to bed, eating supper, or in this case taking a bath.

Hobbits love Mushrooms.

Chapter title refers to Sam, Pippin, and Merry seemingly two fold. It seems due to their closeness to Frodo, they surmised that Frodo was planning to leave the Shire and embark on a journey that he would not return. Merry had once seen Bilbo vanish into thin air to avoid some unruly neighbors and then reappear with a glint of a gold chained ring hastily pocketed. That is how he realized the ring might be magical and important to Frodo's quest. Sam too would often overhear Frodo lamenting to himself never seeing the Shire again; it appears Frodo was less subtle about his quest than he thought. The quest to defeat the Enemy was the conspiracy they believed in. Secondly, they conspired to not only assist Frodo leave the Shire, but also travel with him, by preparing the tools and equipment for a quest before Frodo even got to his new residence so they could leave with haste.

Oh cool, Pippin begins singing another song, this time about leaving to go on a journey, a song that is very reminiscent of the one the dwarves sang to Bilbo on his journey. I guess he really does have a song for every occasion.

The chapter ends ominously.

Frodo has a strange dream. He is dreaming of the sea, he hears the waves crash and smells the salt in the air. It is a dream he has had often in the past. But it is most peculiar as he has never been to the sea because he has never left the Shire before. Frodo sees a white tower on a high ridge in his dream. He is unsure why but he is drawn to it. He feels a strong urge to scale the tower.

What does the dream mean, has he had these dreams before he acquired the ring from Bilbo? It is strange and curious, but the significance will be revealed in time.

Allan Gao
